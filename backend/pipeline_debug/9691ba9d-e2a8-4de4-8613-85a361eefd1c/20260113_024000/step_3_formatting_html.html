The national government has voiced opposition to a proposed 10.9% fare increase for TransMilenio in Bogota, arguing that the hike, set to raise fares to $3,550, is not sufficiently justified solely by the minimum wage increase 1 . The Ministry of Transportation has requested a thorough technical analysis to support the decision 2 . In the labor sector, significant changes are expected in 2026 due to the Labor Reform, which will alter the payment of severance interest for millions of Colombian workers, aiming to facilitate financial planning for both employees and companies 4 .
