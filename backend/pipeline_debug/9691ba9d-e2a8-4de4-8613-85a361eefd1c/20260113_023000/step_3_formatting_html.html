Labor Market and Economic Adjustments
The Colombian labor market is navigating significant changes at the start of 2026, primarily driven by a substantial 23% increase in the minimum wage 1 . This rise is reconfiguring payroll expenses and initiating internal adjustments across businesses, posing difficult decisions for both companies and workers 1 . In parallel, a new Labor Reform is set to alter the payment of severance interest starting in 2026. This reform aims to facilitate periodic income management and enhance financial planning for millions of Colombian workers and their employers 2 . The reform allows for negotiated payment modalities, offering greater flexibility in financial strategies 2 .

Trade Policy and International Relations
Recent U.S. trade policy decisions, specifically the announcement of a 25% tariff on Iran's partners by Donald Trump, are prompting Latin American governments, including Colombia, to re-evaluate their established export routes and partnerships 3 . This development underscores the interconnectedness of global trade and the need for adaptive export strategies in response to geopolitical shifts 3 . In separate foreign policy discussions, President Gustavo Petro convened the Advisory Commission on Foreign Relations to address key issues concerning border security and international cooperation, with specific discussions expected regarding Venezuela and the United States 5 6 . Furthermore, Petro issued a warning to the ELN, indicating that joint military actions with Venezuela would occur if the group does not engage in the peace process and leave Venezuelan territory 7 .

Government Scrutiny on Public Services
The Ministry of Transportation has officially requested a technical explanation for the recent 10.9% fare increase for Bogota's TransMilenio public transport system 4 8 . The government contends that the proposed hike, raising the fare from $3,200 to $3,550 pesos, is not adequately justified by the minimum wage increase alone, despite the system's arguments that it is linked to a 23% rise in the minimum wage 4 8 . This action highlights governmental oversight on public utility pricing and its impact on citizens 4 8 .

Economic Outlook and Sectoral Performance
The broader economic outlook for the country in 2026 is marked by uncertainty, influenced by upcoming parliamentary and presidential elections 9 . While recent manufacturing data for September 2025 showed positive growth in production (5.2%) and sales (5.3%) compared to the previous year, a shift from negative rates, the overall sentiment remains cautious 9 . Industry and commerce sectors face a period with more questions than concrete answers regarding future performance 9 . Inflationary trends are also a key concern.
