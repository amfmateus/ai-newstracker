Economic and Labor Market Developments
The government has expressed strong reservations regarding the recent 10.9% fare increase for TransMilenio in Bogota 2 . The Ministry of Transportation has requested a technical explanation, arguing that the increase, set to raise the fare to $3,550, is not adequately justified by the minimum wage hike alone, despite the system's claims that it was based on the 23% increase in the minimum wage for 2026 1 . This dispute highlights ongoing tensions between national and local authorities on economic policy implementation. Concurrently, the Colombian labor market is facing significant adjustments following a substantial 23% increase in the minimum wage for 2026 3 . Experts predict that this hike will trigger internal salary adjustments across various levels, potentially creating difficulties for businesses and impacting the formal employment sector. The base monthly income, including transportation assistance, now reaches $2 million, increasing labor costs for companies 3 . In broader economic trends, inflation in OECD countries stood at 3.9% in November, with food prices contributing to a decrease while energy prices saw a slight increase 8 . Notably, Costa Rica was the only OECD member with negative inflation 8 . The European Central Bank reported that food prices, including coffee, tea, cocoa, sweets, and meat, surged in 2025 due to climate change, significantly impacting annual food inflation 9 .

Labor Reform and Severance Payments
The implementation of the Labor Reform in Colombia introduces a change in how severance interest is paid starting in 2026 10 . This reform allows for agreements between employees and companies to establish payment modalities that facilitate periodic income management and improve financial planning 10 .

Trade Policy and International Relations
Recent trade policy shifts, specifically US tariffs on Iran's partners, are prompting Latin American governments, including Colombia, to reassess their established export routes and partnerships 4 . This external policy decision necessitates strategic re-evaluation within regional trade dynamics. President Gustavo Petro has convened the Advisory Commission on Foreign Relations to discuss critical issues such as border security and international cooperation, with specific attention to relations with Venezuela and the United States 6 11 . The commission aims to address key foreign policy matters and coordinate approaches to regional challenges. In a significant development regarding internal security and international cooperation, President Petro issued a warning to the ELN guerrilla group. He stated that if the ELN does not join the peace process and withdraw from Venezuelan territory, Colombia will pursue joint military operations with Venezuela 7 . This ultimatum underscores the government's stance on armed groups operating across borders and its commitment to peace negotiation conditional on adherence to established protocols 12 . The ELN has responded by proposing a 'national agreement' amidst these potential joint actions 12 .

President Gustavo Petro has been engaged in political discourse concerning energy rates, criticizing former presidents Álvaro Uribe and Iván Duque regarding past decisions 5 . These exchanges highlight ongoing political debates over energy policy and its historical management. Fiscal and Regulatory Changes
New regulations in Colombia will impact the taxation of pensions in 2026, altering how these benefits are assessed 18 . The modification is tied to the new UVT (Unit of Taxable Value) established by the Dian (Tax Administration Authority), changing exemption calculations and automatic adjustments for retirement and professional risk payments 18 . The Ministry of Health plans to increase its payroll by $59.403 million by 2026, creating 442 new formal positions amidst a crisis within the health system 19 . This move comes in response to a petition and aims to address workload analysis and institutional reorganization 19 .
