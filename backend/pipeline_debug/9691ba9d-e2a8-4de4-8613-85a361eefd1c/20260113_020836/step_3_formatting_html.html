Labor Market Adjustments Amid Minimum Wage Increase
The Colombian labor market is navigating a significant shock at the start of 2026 due to a substantial 23% increase in the minimum wage 1 . This rise has reconfigured payroll costs and initiated internal adjustments across the salary structure, creating a 'domino effect' on employment sustainability 1 . Experts suggest this will lead to difficult decisions for both businesses and workers, impacting the formal employment landscape 1 .

Government Challenges Public Transport Fare Hike
The National Government, through the Ministry of Transportation, has questioned the justification for a $350 fare increase in TransMilenio, Bogotá's public transport system 2 . While TransMilenio cited a 10.9% increase due to the minimum wage hike, the government argues that the minimum wage adjustment does not warrant such a fare hike 2 . This dispute highlights tensions between public service operators and national economic policy objectives.
