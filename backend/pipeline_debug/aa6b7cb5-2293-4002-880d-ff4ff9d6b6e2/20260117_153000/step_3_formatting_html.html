Trade Relations and International Agreements
The Mercosur and the European Union are nearing the signing of a landmark trade agreement, marking the culmination of over 25 years of negotiations 1 . This agreement, intended to increase trade and mitigate the impact of US protectionism, has received approval from key regional blocs despite some criticisms 1 . The pact aims to reduce tariffs and foster greater economic integration between the two major trading blocs, signaling a potentially significant shift in global trade dynamics. The participation of leaders from Argentina, Paraguay, Uruguay, and the President of the European Commission, Ursula von der Leyen, underscores the strategic importance of this accord 1 .

Security and Internal Affairs
The security situation in Colombia is a growing concern, with former minister José Manuel Restrepo warning of a "terrifying deterioration" in security between 2022 and 2025, citing increases in kidnapping, terrorism, and extortion 2 . President Gustavo Petro has also condemned the systematic killing of peasants in the Catatumbo region by the ELN as a crime against humanity, highlighting the ongoing crisis that has victimized over 105,000 people in the past year 7 , 5 ]. The ELN's activities, including drone attacks resulting in military casualties 8 , continue to destabilize the region. In response to rising security issues and to support economic development, the government is advancing key infrastructure projects, such as the Transversal del Catatumbo, with an investment exceeding $1.1 trillion 3 , and a significant electrical works plan in the Caribbean region, valued at over US$1.7 billion, to improve energy services and integrate renewable sources 4 . Additionally, there are reports of the expansion of illegal armed groups threatening upcoming elections 9 .

Economic Policy and Investment Climate
The Colombian economy is facing critical challenges in 2026, with concerns about insufficient GDP growth, a substantial fiscal deficit of 7.1% of GDP, and stagnant foreign direct investment 10 . The government's economic emergency decree is also under scrutiny, facing potential opposition from the Constitutional Court and defiance from several governors, creating uncertainty around revenue collection 11 , 12 ]. The Ministry of Mines and Energy announced a plan to reduce gasoline prices from February 1st, alongside a US$1.7 billion investment in electrical infrastructure for the Caribbean region to improve reliability and integrate renewables 13 . The construction guild, Camacol, has voiced concerns that a decree aiming to de-index social interest housing (VIS) could lead to artificial price controls, reduced supply, and increased costs . In the financial sector, profits rose by 8% to $122 trillion by November 2025, indicating a recovery, with Bancolombia leading earnings 14 . Furthermore, Colombia ranks 36th globally for its startup ecosystem, demonstrating strong growth in fintech, proptech, and digital commerce 15 . Certifications for Puerto Antioquia as a safe port are a positive development for international trade . Meanwhile, the DIAN has announced tax benefits within the economic emergency decree, aimed at alleviating the fiscal crisis 16 .
