Economic Emergency Decree Faces Legal and Political Opposition
President Gustavo Petro's economic emergency decree is facing significant opposition and legal challenges from various regional authorities 1 . Governors from at least 17 departments have raised concerns about the decree's legal viability, questioning whether territorial authorities can disregard a norm with the force of law 1 . Governors such as Rafaela Cortés of Meta have explicitly stated that the decree "affects the finances of the departments" 2 , contributing to a broader "rebellion" against the government's economic measures 6 . The potential intervention of the Constitutional Court, coupled with the defiance of several governors, has placed the collection of funds related to the economic emergency in a state of suspense 3 . This situation highlights a tension between central government authority and departmental autonomy 1 .

Gubernatorial Concerns Over Decree's Financial and Legal Implications
Governors have voiced strong opinions regarding the economic emergency decree. Rafaela Cortés, governor of Meta, articulated concerns that the decree negatively impacts departmental finances, contributing to the widespread "rebellion" 2 . Similarly, Andrés Julián Rendón, governor of Antioquia, has spoken out about this "rebellion" against the government's decree 6 . The debate centers on whether subnational entities can legally disregard a norm issued with the force of law, raising questions about territorial autonomy 1 . Pressures on Small Businesses
Small businesses are experiencing considerable pressure at the beginning of the year. This is largely attributed to a significant 23% increase in the minimum wage, rising surcharges, and a reduction in working hours 5 . This confluence of factors is impacting operational costs and profitability for these enterprises 5 . A legal challenge has been filed against the decree that formalized this 23% minimum wage increase, arguing that the government did not adhere to established legal criteria for wage adjustments 8 .
