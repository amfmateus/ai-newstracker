Economic Impact of Minimum Wage Increase
The recent 23% increase in Colombia's minimum wage for 2026 is a central point of economic discussion and political contention. Experts are highlighting the significant impact this will have on business costs, particularly for small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) 7 . The private security sector anticipates substantial cost increases that could be passed on to consumers 2 . Furthermore, the government faces increased expenses related to pensions, health services, and subsidies due to the higher wage baseline 4 . There are also concerns that this hike could contribute to inflationary pressures 8 . President Petro has taken a firm stance against companies perceived to be irrationally increasing prices in response to the wage hike, with the Superintendency of Industry and Commerce (SIC) slated to investigate such practices to protect consumer rights and market fairness 3 . The labor market is experiencing adjustments as businesses manage increased payroll costs, forcing difficult decisions for both employers and employees 1 . A proposed government decree to de-link housing prices from the minimum wage has also sparked debate, with potential risks to housing supply and affordability 9 , 10 .

Political Reactions and Government Actions
The economic adjustments are drawing significant political scrutiny. President Gustavo Petro has been actively responding to criticisms and issuing directives. He has publicly criticized private health entities (EPS) for prioritizing profits over worker well-being 11 . In response to concerns about price increases, Petro announced government investigations into companies, emphasizing consumer protection 3 . The President also directly addressed critics regarding housing policy, attributing issues to interest rates rather than minimum wage links . Political figures from opposition parties have voiced strong criticism. Senator Mauricio Gómez Amín criticized the government's decision to transfer criminal leaders to Barranquilla, citing security concerns 12 . Former President Álvaro Uribe has called for an investigation into a multi-billion peso contract signed by the presidency for Petro's defense, demanding transparency 13 . Concerns have also been raised about alleged political pressures on RTVC contractors to ensure job continuity and political support 14 . The Auditor General's Office has presented its 'electoral peace' strategy to safeguard upcoming elections 15 .
