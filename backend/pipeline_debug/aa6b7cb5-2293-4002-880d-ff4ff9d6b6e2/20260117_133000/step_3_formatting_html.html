EU-Mercosur Trade Agreement Nears Historic Milestone
The European Union and Mercosur are on the verge of signing a historic trade agreement, culminating over 25 years of negotiations. The deal aims to boost trade between the two blocs and counteract the growing trend of US protectionism. Despite facing some criticism from within both regions, the agreement is viewed as crucial for economic stability. Attending the signing ceremony in Asunción are the presidents of Argentina, Paraguay, and Uruguay, along with the President of the European Commission, Ursula von der Leyen. Bolivia and Panama will also be present as guests. Brazilian President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva will be absent from the event 1 . This agreement is expected to significantly reduce tariffs, thereby fostering increased commercial exchange between Europe and South America 1 .

Global Economic Landscape Marked by Rising Competition and Risks
The World Economic Forum's Global Risks Report 2026 signals a shift towards an era of intensified global competition, where trade, finance, and technology are increasingly employed as instruments of power. This dynamic is raising significant risks for international markets and investment 2 . The report, based on the responses of over 1,300 experts worldwide, highlights that 50% of surveyed individuals perceive the global economy as being in a precarious state 2 . In a related development, President Donald Trump announced a 10% tariff on several European countries and Finland, effective February 1st, with a potential increase to 25% by June if no agreement is reached regarding Greenland. This measure underscores the growing trend of economic protectionism and heightened trade tensions 4 . Against this backdrop, the OECD has reported historically high employment levels and stable unemployment at 5% across its member countries in Q3 2025, albeit with notable variations between nations 6 .

Economic Outlook and Sectoral Developments in Colombia
Colombia's economic outlook for 2026 indicates a gradual decrease in inflation, though certain sectors such as education and housing are expected to face increased inflationary pressures 3 . The Colombian economy is growing, but concerns remain about insufficient investment and productivity to sustain formal employment . The fiscal panorama is also a point of concern, with a projected deficit of 7.1% of GDP for 2025, which could lead to higher debt costs and reduced public investment . In the energy sector, the National Government has launched an electrical works plan exceeding US$1.37 billion to enhance energy services in the Caribbean region. This initiative includes over 35 strategic works aimed at strengthening the grid, preventing blackouts, and integrating 6,000 megawatts of clean energy 5 . Small businesses are facing significant financial pressure at the start of the year due to a 23% increase in the minimum wage, rising surcharges, and reduced working hours 7 . Furthermore, a legal challenge has been filed against the government's decree setting the 2026 minimum wage at $2,000,000, questioning its legality and constitutionality 8 . In the financial sector, profits rose by 8% to $122 trillion by November 2025, indicating a sector-wide recovery, with Bancolombia leading in earnings 9 . Meanwhile, Celsia Colombia's shareholders approved the delisting of its ordinary shares from the Colombia Stock Exchange due to low liquidity and high share concentration 10 .

Infrastructure and Investment Developments
The National Government is implementing a significant infrastructure plan with an investment of over US$1.37 billion aimed at improving energy services in the Caribbean region 5 . This project includes over 35 strategic works designed to strengthen the electrical grid, mitigate blackout risks, and facilitate the integration of 6,000 megawatts of clean energy, particularly from wind and solar sources 5 . In the transportation sector, the Ministry of Transport is updating the SICE-TAC system, a tool for calculating freight transport costs, by incorporating new variables such as the minimum wage and fuel prices. This update aims to provide more accurate and verifiable cost data for transporters 11 . Additionally, toll rates on major Colombian roads have been adjusted, with some increases surpassing the 5.10% inflation rate 12 . Puerto Antioquia has received its safety certification from Dimar, positioning it to commence international trade operations . The Aerocafé airport project in Caldas is set to begin construction on its air side in February, with completion expected in 2029, while efforts continue to secure funding for the land side terminal 13 .

Political and Security Landscape
President Gustavo Petro's administration is facing scrutiny over a 10 billion peso contract with foreign lawyers for his defense related to the Clinton List, prompting an investigation by the Prosecutor's Office 14 . The ongoing conflict in Catatumbo has resulted in a severe humanitarian crisis, with over 105,000 people victimized in 2025 due to armed confrontations between the ELN and dissident groups, marked by mass displacements and violence 15 . An ELN drone attack in Tibú killed one soldier and injured four others during army operations in the region 16 . President Petro condemned these attacks, highlighting the casualties and questioning the continued conflict 17 . Former President Álvaro Uribe criticized President Petro's upcoming meeting with Donald Trump, labeling Petro and Senator Iván Cepeda as allies of Nicolás Maduro 18 . In a separate political development, the Prosecutor's Office has opened an investigation into a contract with a law firm advising Petro in the US, raising questions about potential irregularities 19 . Regional leaders, including governors from Antioquia and Valle del Cauca, are expressing concerns about the executive's economic emergency decree, questioning its legal viability and potential impact on territorial autonomy 20 . The President of the Senate, Lidio García, has also voiced opposition to proposed budget cuts for Congress, arguing they could compromise its functionality 21 . Regarding security, Civil Aeronautics has dismissed concerns stemming from a US warning about Pacific airspace activities, stating no evidence of threats to air navigation 22 . The FAA has issued a safety alert for the Colombian Pacific airspace due to reported military activities and GNSS interference 23 .

Economic Policy and Regulatory Developments
The government's economic policies are under scrutiny, with the Council of State admitting a lawsuit challenging the decree that set the 2026 minimum wage at $2,000,000, based on arguments that legal criteria were not followed 8 . The construction guild, Camacol, warns that the maximum price for social interest housing has increased by 76% under the current government, arguing that price controls are counterproductive and reduce supply 24 . The Ministry of Mines and Energy announced a reduction in gasoline prices effective February 1st, aiming to stabilize fuel costs and avoid new debt in the Fuel Price Stabilization Fund (FEPC) 25 . The Ministry is also updating the SICE-TAC system for freight transport cost calculation, incorporating new variables like the minimum wage and fuel prices to provide transporters with more accurate data 11 . In the healthcare sector, the National Superintendency of Health has extended a precautionary measure against Fomag due to ongoing service failures, requiring an updated work plan to address these issues 26 . The Attorney General's Office has also alerted Nueva EPS regarding persistent failures in drug delivery and has demanded improvements to their contingency plan to ensure timely supply to affiliates 27 . The State Council has ruled that EPS can utilize UPC funds to settle outstanding debts, provided certain conditions are met 28 . Regarding taxation, the government plans to raise approximately $2.5 trillion through increased VAT on tobacco and spirits, a move that has faced opposition from 20 of the country's 32 governors 29 .
