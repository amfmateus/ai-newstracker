Colombia Grapples with Minimum Wage Impact, Political Tensions, and Economic Policy

Executive Summary

Colombia's economic landscape is significantly shaped by the recent 23% increase in the minimum wage, impacting labor costs and business operations across sectors. Chef Leonor Espinosa warns of rising food prices affecting both everyday meals and high-end dining 1. Small and medium-sized enterprises face critical financial decisions due to increased payroll and economic uncertainty 2. The labor market is undergoing adjustments, with potential wage compression affecting the entire salary structure 3. Politically, criticism mounts over government actions, including a controversial contract for President Petro's defense 4, alleged political pressures on public contractors 5, and transfers of criminal leaders to Barranquilla, sparking security concerns 6. The Prosecutor's Office is promoting an 'electoral peace' strategy to ensure fair elections 7. In international relations, dialogue between Colombia and the US has improved, potentially leading to the elimination of steel tariffs 8.

Economic Impact of Minimum Wage Increase
The recent 23% increase in Colombia's minimum wage is creating significant ripple effects across the economy. Businesses are facing heightened payroll costs and are compelled to undertake internal adjustments, potentially leading to wage compression throughout the salary structure 3. Small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) are particularly vulnerable, needing to make strategic financial decisions amid economic uncertainty and higher operational costs 2. Chef Leonor Espinosa has warned that rising input costs will inevitably translate to higher food prices for both basic meals and fine dining establishments 1. Restaurants are already contemplating closures, menu modifications, and layoffs as a direct consequence of increased labor expenses and inflation 9. The daily meal, known as 'corrientazo,' is also predicted to see a price increase 10. Meanwhile, the National Institute of Statistics and Censuses (INDEC) will release December inflation data, with annual inflation expected to be around 30% for Argentina 11, and Colombia's annual inflation closed 2025 at 5.10% 12.

Political Developments and Criticisms
Political discourse is dominated by criticism of government actions and upcoming elections. Senator Mauricio Gómez Amín has voiced strong opposition to the transfer of criminal structures to Barranquilla, citing negative impacts on citizen security and the upcoming carnival season 6. This sentiment is echoed by presidential candidate De la Espriella, who labeled the transfer as "criminal" and warned of empowered criminals intimidating citizens 13. Mayor Alejandro Char has also criticized President Petro's administration for this decision, seeking better coordination with the national government 14. Concerns have been raised about a substantial 10 billion peso contract signed by the presidency for President Petro's legal defense, with Álvaro Uribe and Katherine Miranda calling for investigations and transparency 15, 16. Enrique Gómez has also strongly criticized Petro and his lawyer over this contract 17. Additionally, alleged political pressures on RTVC contractors, linking job continuity to political support, have been denounced by councilman Daniel F. Briceño 5. The Prosecutor's Office is actively socializing its 'electoral peace' strategy across the country to safeguard the integrity of upcoming elections 7. President Petro himself responded to criticism from former Housing Minister Luis Felipe Henao on housing policy, identifying interest rates as the core problem 18. Meanwhile, other political figures like Ingrid Betancourt have questioned Petro's silence on the repression of women in Iran 19. President Petro also defended his anti-drug policy, noting reduced raw material values and increased seizures 20, and addressed criticism from doctors regarding salary increases, also targeting private health entities (EPS) for prioritizing profits 21.

Electoral Integrity and Political Maneuvering
The Prosecutor's Office is implementing an 'electoral peace' strategy to ensure secure and fair elections by engaging citizens and preventing irregularities 7. The National Registrar is also proposing a reform to prevent citizens from signing petitions for multiple candidates, limiting support to a single candidate 22. Presidential candidate Abelardo de la Espriella leads a recent poll for the 2026 elections, ahead of Iván Cepeda and Sergio Fajardo 23.

International Relations and Trade
Diplomatic engagement between Colombia and the United States has shown signs of improvement, potentially paving the way for the elimination of steel and aluminum tariffs 8. Following a period of tension, recent dialogue has fostered a more cordial relationship, opening opportunities for bilateral trade. In other international news, US President Donald Trump's focus on Greenland continues, with reports suggesting a potential acquisition cost of around $4.4 trillion, despite Denmark's refusal to sell . A European Commissioner warned that a forced US takeover of Greenland could mean the end of NATO 24. The meeting between Trump and Venezuelan opposition leader María Corina Machado at the White House is scheduled for January 15, addressing Venezuela's political situation 25, 26. Italy is set to enhance relations with Venezuela following the release of two Italian citizens 27. Brazil has announced it will return diplomatic representation of Argentina in Venezuela 28. In global technology, Google and Apple have entered a multi-year agreement for Google to provide AI technology, including powering Siri 29, 30. The 2026 World Cup is anticipated to be the most technologically advanced in history, integrating artificial intelligence across all aspects of the tournament 31.

Financial Markets and Economic Indicators
Gold and silver prices have reached record highs, exceeding $4,600 per ounce and $85 per ounce respectively, following a US Federal Reserve investigation 32, 33. The price of gold has seen a significant appreciation in 2026, following a strong performance in 2025 33. Wall Street experienced a downturn as President Trump intensified his criticism of the Federal Reserve, raising concerns about central bank independence 34. Analysts expect the Central Bank's key interest rate to remain at 4.5% in January, with a potential 25 basis point cut anticipated in March, alongside inflation projected around 3% 35. The Colombian peso has demonstrated volatility in early 2026, influenced by both external factors like US inflation data and internal economic conditions 36. The peso might strengthen mid-January due to rising oil prices and attractive investor rates 37. The euro's closing price in Colombia on January 12 showed an increase compared to the previous day 38. The dollar's opening value in Colombia on January 12 is also reported 39.

Government Policies and Sectoral Impact
The Petro government's housing policies are facing scrutiny and debate. A new proposal to de-link housing prices from the minimum wage, setting a maximum value for Social Interest Housing (VIS), has sparked concerns about potential negative impacts on supply and prices, particularly for lower-income families 40, 41. Minister Antonio Sanguino defended these policies, emphasizing pricing in Colombian pesos and transparency to prevent abuses against the poor . However, critics like Carolina Arbeláez dispute the government's approach, warning of reduced VIS supply and price increases 42. The Ministry of Health is planning to create 442 new public sector jobs, projecting an annual cost of $59.403 million by 2026, amidst a crisis in the healthcare system 43. In agricultural matters, farmers and poultry producers in Cáqueza are protesting low prices that fail to cover production costs, blocking a key road and urging government intervention 44. Grupo Éxito has launched a back-to-school campaign offering discounts and a wide range of school supplies to support Colombian families 45. The Ministry of Education defended the president regarding inflation-adjusted tariffs, suggesting prices can be instruments of power 46.
