Inflation stable, housing policy debated, and international markets react to US Fed probe

Executive Summary

Colombia's annual inflation rate concluded 2025 at 5.10%, a slight change from the previous year, with December's monthly inflation recorded at 0.27% (1). In economic policy, a proposed government decree aims to price Social Interest Housing (VIS) in Colombian pesos rather than minimum wage units, a move intended to increase transparency and prevent automatic price hikes, though it faces debate regarding potential impacts on supply and existing contracts (2) (3). Internationally, gold and silver prices reached record highs following a US Department of Justice investigation into the Federal Reserve, sparking concerns about central bank independence and potential interest rate cuts (4) (5).

Economic and Monetary Developments
Colombia's inflation rate for 2025 concluded at 5.10%, showing minimal change from the previous year, with monthly inflation in December registering at 0.27%, slightly below analyst expectations (1). Analysts anticipate the Central Bank will maintain its key interest rate at 4.5% in January but expect a 25 basis point cut in March, with inflation projected to be around 3% (7). In the broader economic landscape, the proposed government decree to de-link housing prices from the minimum wage by setting a maximum value for Social Interest Housing (VIS) has generated significant debate regarding its potential economic and financial consequences (8) (3). The Ministry of Housing's draft decree aims to ensure VIS are priced in Colombian pesos, not indexed to the minimum wage, and enhance buyer protection (2) (9). This move is intended to prevent automatic price adjustments tied to minimum wage increases (2). The 23% increase in the minimum wage for 2026 is also expected to affect other sectors, with restaurants warning of potential closures and layoffs due to increased payroll costs (6) (10).

International Markets and Geopolitics
Global markets reacted to a US Department of Justice investigation into the Federal Reserve, leading to record highs for gold and silver prices, reaching $4,600 per ounce and $85 per ounce, respectively (4) (5). The investigation has raised concerns about the central bank's autonomy and potential interest rate cuts in the United States (5) (11). Wall Street experienced a downturn as President Trump intensified his criticism of the Federal Reserve, contributing to a growing demand for safe-haven assets (11). Meanwhile, in a significant diplomatic development, U.S. President Donald Trump is scheduled to meet with Venezuelan opposition leader María Corina Machado at the White House on January 15th, following previous critical remarks by Trump and concerning the political situation in Venezuela (12) (13). Italy is also set to improve relations with Venezuela following the release of two Italian citizens, Alberto Trentini and Mario Burlo (14). The European Commissioner for Defense commented that a forced U.S. takeover of Greenland would lead to the end of NATO, emphasizing EU members' obligation to defend Denmark against aggression (15).

Political and Governance Landscape
Colombian President Gustavo Petro's government has faced criticism regarding recent personnel changes and payroll increases within public institutions, with watchdog groups expressing concern over potential electoral motives and management issues (16). Carlos Carrillo, director of UNGRD, has accused Angie Rodríguez, director of Dapre, of planning layoffs to favor allies before the Law of Guarantees takes effect, and also of appointing friends and family to the Adaptation Fund (17) (18). Political figures like Álvaro Uribe and Katherine Miranda are demanding an investigation into a controversial 10 billion peso defense contract signed by the Presidency for President Petro's legal defense, arguing for transparency (19) (20) (21). The ELN has proposed a "national agreement" aimed at institutionalizing measures against poverty and corruption, intending to integrate these into the electoral process, possibly in response to potential joint security actions between the US and Colombia (22) (23). President Petro responded to criticism from Barranquilla's mayor, Alejandro Char, regarding the transfer of criminal leaders, asserting that his administration has reduced the city's homicide rate (24) (25) (26).

Trade and Investment Outlook
Diplomatic dialogue between Colombia and the United States has improved following a period of tension, potentially paving the way for the elimination of tariffs on products such as steel and aluminum (27). The global race for artificial intelligence is driving demand for resources like energy, semiconductors, and minerals, reshaping geopolitical priorities . In the retail sector, Grupo Éxito has launched a back-to-school campaign offering discounts and a wide selection of school supplies, valid until February 28th (28). Parque Arauco is advancing its open innovation strategy in Colombia with technology pilots planned for its Parque La Colina shopping center in Bogotá, following the selection of five startups for its Startup Challenge program (29).
