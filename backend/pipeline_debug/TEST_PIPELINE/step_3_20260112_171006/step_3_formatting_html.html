Economic Policy, Security Concerns, and Housing Regulations Dominate Colombian News

Executive Summary

The economic landscape in Colombia is shaped by a significant minimum wage increase and its ripple effects, including potential impacts on administration fees and consumer prices 1 . Experts predict shifts in key economic indicators for 2026, with interest rates expected to stabilize around 9.25% . In housing policy, the government is introducing measures to de-index Social Interest Housing (VIS) prices from the minimum wage and set new pricing regulations 2 . Security remains a concern, with reports of coordinated military operations against the ELN involving Colombia, the US, and Venezuela 3 , and ongoing discussions about international relations and drug trafficking 4 .

Economic Policy and Financial Outlook
Colombia's economic forecast for 2026 indicates expected shifts in key indicators. Experts surveyed by Fedesarrollo anticipate interest rates to conclude the year at 9.25%, with a slightly higher 9.50% expected in the first quarter . The country faces ongoing challenges in managing inflation, with reports suggesting it is difficult to bring inflation back to target ranges 5 . The recent 23% increase in the minimum wage is expected to have broad economic repercussions, impacting various sectors 6 . In response to the minimum wage hike, the government is implementing measures to address its impact on household finances. The Ministry of Housing has published a draft decree aimed at preventing Social Interest Housing (VIS) prices from being indexed to the minimum wage, requiring prices to be fixed in Colombian pesos 2 7 . This initiative seeks to protect buyers and avoid automatic price increases not tied to actual construction costs . Furthermore, President Gustavo Petro has urged property owner assemblies to manage administration fees prudently, basing increases on inflation rather than solely the minimum wage 1 8 9 . The restaurant sector, in particular, is feeling the pressure from increased minimum wage and consumer price index (CPI) costs, leading to considerations of closures, menu changes, and layoffs 10 .

Security and International Relations
Security and international relations remain prominent themes, with reports of a potential coordinated military operation against the ELN involving Colombia, the United States, and Venezuela 3 . The Ministry of Defense has highlighted the security implications for Colombia stemming from the indefinite presence of the US in Venezuela 11 . Discussions also touch upon strengthening relations with the US to combat drug trafficking more effectively 4 . In diplomatic spheres, Colombian President Gustavo Petro's stance on US actions in Venezuela and his exchange with Donald Trump are noted, with Chancellor Rosa Villavicencio stating shared interests between Colombia, the US, and Venezuela regarding the latter's well-being . Juan Guaidó has criticized Petro's position on US actions following Nicolás Maduro's capture, labeling it as hypocritical 12 . The political situation in Venezuela, including alleged wealth accumulation by regime leaders, continues to draw international attention . Domestically, political discourse includes criticism of a significant government contract for President Petro's legal defense, with opposition figures questioning the use of state funds 13 14 15 . The upcoming congressional elections are also a point of focus, with concerns raised about potential terrorist influence and the need for broad political support 16 . The Attorney General's Office is initiating a national awareness campaign on 'electoral peace' 17 .

Legal and Criminal Proceedings
Legal proceedings and criminal activities are also reported. An alleged member of the ELN was captured following an attack that resulted in military casualties . The case of a VAT cartel saw 11 arrests following a raid on shell companies 18 . Furthermore, a suspected European drug trafficker was apprehended in Santa Marta operating with a false Colombian identity 19 , and another Swiss citizen wanted for drug trafficking in Spain was arrested in Santa Marta 20 . In judicial matters, former paramilitary leaders' testimonies were highlighted in the conviction of Jorge Visbal, ex-president of Fedegán 21 . The president of the JEP commented on the implications of dismantling the court, suggesting it would lead to impunity for victims and cast doubt on future peace processes 22 .

Infrastructure and Urban Development
Progress in infrastructure projects is noted, with the Urban Development Institute (IDU) achieving its highest budget execution in over a decade in 2025, securing funding for Bogotá projects 23 . The completion of the concrete structure for a key vehicular bridge, part of Line 1 of the Bogotá Metro, was announced, improving mobility in the city 24 . Meanwhile, a new decree is proposed to set a maximum price for Social Interest Housing (VIS) and regulate pricing practices in pesos, aiming to protect buyers 25 .
