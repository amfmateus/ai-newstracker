Economic Landscape and Financial Sector Performance
Colombia's financial system demonstrated robust performance by November 2025, with total returns to clients reaching BCOP 122.08 trillion, an 8.06% increase from the previous year 1 . Credit institutions and banks led this growth, with Bancolombia reporting BCOP 5.68 trillion in profits, followed by Banco Davivienda with BCOP 1.50 trillion 7 . Only four out of 30 reported banks experienced losses, indicating a sector-wide recovery 8 . In parallel, the startup ecosystem continues to expand, with Colombia ranking 36th globally and second in South America 6 . Meantime, market indicators show the US dollar experienced a slight weekly gain of $56, closing at $3,685.00 on Friday, January 16th, with a bullish global outlook expected to sustain its strength 9 . However, concerns linger regarding the economic impact of government policies, particularly the 23% minimum wage increase and its potential strain on small businesses 10 , and the fiscal challenges posed by a projected 7.1% GDP deficit for 2025 11 .

Infrastructure and Transportation Policy
Significant infrastructure development is underway, with the Aerocafé airport project in Caldas advancing towards construction of its air side in February, aiming for a 2029 completion 12 . The Ministry of Transport is updating the SICE-TAC system with new cost variables, including minimum wage and fuel prices, to enhance accuracy in freight transport cost calculations . Toll rates for Category 1 vehicles have seen increases, with Cisneros, Pipiral, and Aburrá identified as having the highest rates nationally 13 . President Petro's intention to dismantle tolls in the Coffee Axis faces legal hurdles, as a concession contract remains valid until February 2027, prompting a reversion process instead of direct dismantling 14 . The Cundinamarca Governorate is petitioning the Superintendency of Transport to review intermunicipal bus fare increases, seeking to prevent excessive hikes .

Governance and Political Dynamics
Political tensions are evident, with several governors challenging the economic emergency decree issued by President Gustavo Petro, raising questions about territorial autonomy and legal viability 4 . This 'rebellion' from regional leaders, including those from Antioquia and Valle del Cauca, underscores a broader trend of confrontation with the central government on fiscal and security matters 15 . The Senate President, Lidio García, expressed concerns that proposed budget cuts for Congress could compromise its operational capacity and independence 16 . In the healthcare sector, the National Superintendency of Health has extended precautionary measures against Fomag due to persistent failures in teacher healthcare services, necessitating an updated work plan 6 . Furthermore, the Attorney General's Office has alerted Nueva EPS about an increase in complaints regarding drug delivery failures, demanding immediate improvements to its contingency plan 17 .

International Relations and Trade
US President Donald Trump has indicated potential tariffs on countries that do not support his plan for Greenland, citing national security concerns 5 . This stance has drawn criticism from a former NATO chief 18 , and US Senator Lisa Murkowski has stated that Congress possesses tools, particularly regarding federal spending, to counter such actions 19 . The US is also proposing to expand its 'Board of Peace' initiative, initially focused on Gaza, to other global conflict zones 20 . In Latin America, Mexico's President Claudia Sheinbaum responded to US demands on drug trafficking by emphasizing the role of US consumption and public health approaches 21 . Venezuela's interim leader Delcy Rodríguez met with the CIA chief in Caracas, amidst other political developments concerning the Maduro regime 22 .

Sectoral Developments and Corporate News
In the aviation sector, Aerocivil received an alert from the FAA regarding potential military activity and GNSS interference in Colombian airspace, particularly over the Pacific Ocean, advising caution for aircraft operations 23 , 24 . Wingo has officially resumed operations to and from Caracas, Venezuela, after a temporary suspension 25 . Avianca has significantly reduced its carbon intensity by nearly 20% through fleet modernization and efficiency improvements 26 . Celsia Colombia will delist its ordinary shares from the Colombia Stock Exchange due to low liquidity and high share concentration, aiming to reduce costs 27 , 28 . In sports, Supersociedades has placed Deportivo Pereira F.C. S.A. under a control measure due to critical legal, accounting, and administrative issues 29 , 30 , 31 .
